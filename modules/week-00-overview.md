## What students usually struggle with
- Git and GitHub can feel harder than the course content this week. The goal is not speed; it is proving that you can clone, edit, commit, push, and find the same work again.
- If something goes wrong, slow down and repeat one small step rather than restarting your whole setup from scratch. `git status` and `git pull --ff-only` should become your first recovery tools, not your last resort.

## Checkpoint question
Can you clone your repository, make one small change, commit it with a clear message, confirm that the update appears on GitHub, and explain what `git status` and `git pull --ff-only` tell you?
